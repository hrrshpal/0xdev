Misal Pav

Misal Pav is a spicy and flavorful Maharashtrian dish made with sprouted lentils (usually moth beans) cooked in a fiery red curry called "kat" or "tarri". The curry is topped with crunchy farsan (fried snack mix), chopped onions, coriander, and a squeeze of lemon. It is served with buttered pav (bread rolls), making it a delicious and satisfying meal. Steps:

Cooking the Misal (Curry)

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add tomatoes, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt. Cook until tomatoes turn mushy.
  5. Add sprouted moth beans and 2 cups of water. Cover and cook for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add tamarind pulp (optional) and mix well. Simmer for another 5 minutes.

Toasting the Pav

  1. Heat butter on a tawa and toast the pav until golden brown.

Assembling & Serving

  1. Pour the hot misal into a bowl.
  2. Top with farsan, chopped onions, coriander, and lemon juice.
  3. Serve with buttered pav on the side.
