Vada Pav

Vada Pav, often called the "Indian burger," is a popular street food from Mumbai. It consists of a spicy, deep-fried potato vada (fritter) sandwiched between a soft pav (bread roll) and served with spicy garlic chutney, green chilies, and sometimes a tamarind sauce. This dish is a perfect combination of crispy, spicy, and soft textures, making it an all-time favorite snack for Mumbaikars and beyond!

Ingredients:

For the Vada (Potato Fritter)

  • 2 large potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds

For Serving

  • 4 pav (bread rolls)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 green chilies (fried)
  • 2 tbsp dry garlic chutney (optional)

Steps:

Making the Potato Filling

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, and red chili powder. SautÃ© for a minute.
  3. Add mashed potatoes, salt, coriander leaves, and lemon juice. Mix well and let it cool.
  4. Shape the mixture into small round balls.

Making the Batter & Frying

  1. In a bowl, mix besan, salt, baking soda, and water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Dip each potato ball into the batter and deep fry until golden brown. Drain excess oil.

Assembling the Vada Pav

  1. Slice the pav in half and toast it with butter on a tawa.
  2. Spread dry garlic chutney (or green chutney) on the pav.
  3. Place the fried vada inside the pav and press lightly.
  4. Serve with fried green chilies and extra chutney on the side.
